Girl Scouts of Suffolk County Hosts
14th Annual Winter Lights Show to Fund STEM Programs for Girls
By: Megan McGuire, Clare Ramirez-Raftree, and Brittany Davis
















pictures courtesy of Clare Ramirez-Raftree & Girl Scouts of Suffolk County

Hosted at the Smith Point Campgrounds, Suffolk County Executive Steve Bellone is putting on the 14th annual Winter Light Show













in partnership with Girls Scouts of Suffolk County to, in part, fund STEM programs for the girls.

According to to Jacqueline Morgan, the Director of Public Relations for Suffolk County Girl Scouts, most people like to go through the light show more than once or even donate more than the recommended amount when they discover that the proceeds go toward the Suffolk County Girl Scouts.

All the show’s proceeds go towards STEM programs, leadership programs, community outreach, and indoor and outdoor camping programs for the Suffolk County Girl Scouts.

“With our outreach programming we offer Girl Scout activities during the school day to girls who may not have access to troops or after-school activities,” said Morgan.

A few years ago, when Girl Scout membership started declining on a national level, the National Girl Scout Organization started a movement to push their STEM programs.

“We actually have a STEM center at our camp in Yaphank, and that’s where we offer a lot of our special programs,” said Morgan.

According to the Girl Scouts of Suffolk County website,the Discovery STEM Center in Yaphank “offers girls a first-rate, hands-on approach to exploring a whole new world










through experiments and demonstrations, special guest presentations and field trips.”

The preparation for the show begins in November, and usually takes about a week for the camp and maintenance staff to set up the lights, according to Morgan.

The Winter Light Show receives their lights from a private vendor, so every year they display new lights along with old favorites.

This is the second year the Winter Light Show is located at Smith Point, which allows for a larger light show, and, concurrently, more attendees.

According to Elena Lee, the Multimedia Designer, the boost in visitors was due to the show’s change to a larger, more accomodating venue.

Last year, the Winter Light Show had their most lucrative season ever, with about 16,500 cars.

“Every year we seem to do a little bit better,” said Morgan.













“Last year was our best year ever, so this year we’re hoping to do our best.”

“It brings the Suffolk County Community together. It’s a tradition for a lot of families who come every year,” said Morgan.

“It’s just so much fun. You don’t have to do anything. You just drive through and enjoy all the lights, celebrate the holidays.”
